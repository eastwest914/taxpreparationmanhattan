Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About Tax Preparation in Manhattan

We have answered the most common questions from our New York clients below. For anything not covered here, call us at 917-259-2134.

What is the difference between personal and business tax preparation?
Personal tax preparation focuses on individual income tax returns, covering wages, investment income, deductions, and credits. Business tax preparation addresses entity-level returns for sole proprietors, partnerships, LLCs, S-corps, and C-corps, including payroll taxes, depreciation schedules, and business deductions. At Tax Preparation Manhattan, we handle both with equal expertise.
How do I know if I need a certified tax professional?
If you own a business, have multiple income sources, rent out property, received an IRS notice, need to file amended tax returns, or want to maximize your deductions legally, working with a certified tax professional is strongly recommended. Professional guidance typically pays for itself many times over through greater accuracy and smarter planning.
What documents do I need to bring for tax preparation?
Bring your W-2s, 1099s, prior year tax return, Social Security numbers for all dependents, records of deductible expenses such as mortgage interest, charitable donations, and business costs, as well as any correspondence from the IRS or New York State tax authorities. We provide a detailed checklist after you book your appointment.
Can you help if I receive an IRS audit notice?
Absolutely. Our IRS audit help service covers correspondence audits, office audits, and field audits. We review your records, prepare your response, and communicate directly with the IRS on your behalf. You should never respond to an IRS audit without professional representation, and our team has successfully resolved many audit cases for Manhattan clients.
Do you offer year round tax services or only during tax season?
We provide year round tax service for all clients. Beyond the standard April filing deadline, we assist with quarterly estimated tax payments, payroll processing, bookkeeping, financial planning, and proactive tax strategy reviews throughout the year. Staying ahead of your tax obligations year round prevents surprises and often leads to significantly lower annual tax bills.
What are your office hours?
We are open Monday through Friday from 9:00 AM to 7:00 PM and Saturday from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM. We are closed on Sundays. Evening weekday hours make it easy for working professionals in Manhattan to meet with us without disrupting their schedule.
Can you help me file amended tax returns for prior years?
Yes. We handle amended tax returns for prior years, whether you missed deductions on an earlier filing, received corrected tax documents, or need to correct errors from a previous preparer. In many cases, clients receive additional refunds after filing amended returns. Contact us to review your past filings.
